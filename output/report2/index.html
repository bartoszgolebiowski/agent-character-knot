Chapters 1–20

In July 1805, the St. Petersburg and Moscow aristocracies are preoccupied with the looming war against Napoleon and the impending death of the wealthy Count Bezúkhov. At Anna Pávlovna Schérer’s soirée, the socially awkward Pierre Bezúkhov and the disillusioned Prince Andrew Bolkónski emerge as central figures; Andrew decides to leave his pregnant wife, Lise, to serve as an aide-de-camp to General Kutúzov. Meanwhile, in Moscow, the vibrant Rostóv family celebrates a name day, highlighting the youthful romances of Natásha and Borís, and Nicholas and Sónya, before the young men depart for military service. The narrative arc is defined by the contrast between the rigid social maneuvering of Prince Vasíli and Anna Mikháylovna and the authentic, albeit chaotic, search for purpose by Pierre and Andrew. As Count Bezúkhov nears death, the struggle for his inheritance begins, while the younger generation is thrust from domestic stability into the uncertainty of the Napoleonic Wars.
